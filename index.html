Timing Means Everything

Doctors recommend that you wait until you are done having children before considering any type of cosmetic surgery. This is because future pregnancies can undo all the hard work and results achieved from your Adelaide mummy makeover. If you hope to have more children, it's best to wait until after you've given birth to your last child before scheduling any procedure.

Your Weight Matters

If you are overweight or obese, you may not be a good candidate for a mummy makeover Adelaide. The reason is that being overweight can increase your risk for complications during and after surgery. If you are considering a mummy makeover, doctors recommend that you be at a healthy weight before surgery.

A mummy makeover is not a "quick fix" and will not magically erase all the changes that have occurred to your body during pregnancy and motherhood.
Choose a Board-Certified Surgeon

Mind Your Health

Before you commit to any type of surgery, you must see that you're in good overall health. This means that you should be at a healthy weight, have no active medical conditions, and not smoke cigarettes. If you happen to be wary of any health concerns, discuss them with your surgeon before scheduling the Adelaide mummy makeover.

Medical Conditions That Won't Allow the Procedure

Certain medical conditions can increase your risk for complications during and after surgery. These conditions include but are not limited to diabetes, high blood pressure, heart disease, and lung disease. If you suffer from any of these conditions, discuss them with your surgeon before scheduling a mummy makeover procedure Adelaide.

Diabetes - If you have diabetes, your body has difficulty regulating blood sugar levels. This can increase your risk for infection and other complications after surgery.

High Blood Pressure - High blood pressure can make it difficult for your wounds to heal properly after surgery. This can lead to an increased risk for infection.

Heart Disease - Heart disease can make it difficult for your heart to pump blood properly. This potentially can increase your risk for complications during and after surgery.

Lung Disease - Lung disease can make it difficult for you to breathe properly after surgery. This can lead to an increased risk for infection and other complications.

Smoking - Smoking cigarettes can increase your risk for complications during and after Adelaide mummy makeover procedure. It could very well make it difficult for your wounds to heal properly.

Risks and Complications

As with any type of surgery, certain risks and complications can occur with a mummy makeover Adelaide. These likely will include infection, bleeding, blood clots, and adverse reactions to anaesthesia. Be sure to discuss these risks with your surgeon before scheduling surgery.

Infection is one of the most common complications after any type of surgery. Signs of infection will show like redness, swelling, and discharge from your incisions.

Bleeding - Bleeding is another common complication that can occur after surgery. If you experience excessive bleeding, you may need a transfusion.

Blood Clots - Blood clots can form in your legs or lungs after surgery. This can be a potentially life-threatening complication.

Adverse Reactions to Anaesthesia - Some patients may experience an allergic reaction to the anaesthesia used during surgery. Symptoms of an allergic reaction include difficulty breathing, hives, and swelling.
